# Handle Recourse

Recourse means the factor can ask for their advance back if your debtor doesn't pay.

## When Does Recourse Happen?

Recourse only applies if you enabled it when [factoring the invoice](factor-invoice.md). If recourse was **off**, the factor absorbs the loss — you keep the advance no matter what.

If recourse was **on** and the debtor misses the due date, the factor can initiate a recourse claim.

## What You'll See

When a factor initiates recourse, a **Recourse Notice** appears on your dashboard. You'll also receive an email notification if you've linked your email.

The notice shows:
- Which invoice is affected
- How much you need to repay (the original advance amount)
- Which factor is requesting repayment

<!-- screenshot: recourse-notice.png — the recourse notice on the business dashboard -->

## How to Settle

1. Click **Settle Recourse** on the notice
2. Confirm the repayment amount
3. The advance is transferred back to the factor
4. The recourse claim is closed

<!-- screenshot: settle-recourse.png — the settle recourse confirmation -->

## What If You Don't Settle?

The recourse claim remains open on-chain. The factor and business must resolve the dispute outside the platform. ZK Factor records the claim status but does not enforce collection.
